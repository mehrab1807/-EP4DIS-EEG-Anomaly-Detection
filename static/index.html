Educational Hub & Project Background

Based on the EP4DIS Research Project Proposal

1. Introduction to EEG & Data Strategy

Electroencephalography (EEG) and Intracranial EEG (iEEG) measure electrical activity in the brain. Epileptic seizures manifest as chaotic, hyper-synchronous states known as the ictal state, distinct from the normative inter-ictal baseline.

Methodology: This project uses the OpenNeuro ds003029 multicenter dataset. The BIDS-formatted raw data undergoes 1-40Hz band-pass filtering and chronological epoching to perfectly isolate the transition from the inter-ictal baseline to the seizure onset, while mathematically rejecting biological artifacts (like ocular movements).

2. Model Theory & Architecture

ARIMA (Auto-Regressive Integrated Moving Average)

A classic linear model that creates a stationary baseline using differencing. However, its univariate nature often fails to adapt to the fast, chaotic transitions of an ictal event, causing unacceptable false positive rates due to normal artifacts.

ARMAX (ARIMA with Exogenous Inputs)

Models the spatial dynamics of the brain network. By feeding the signal from one electrode as an exogenous 'X' input into a neighboring electrode's equation, ARMAX can detect the spatial propagation of a seizure earlier.

Kalman Filter (Recursive Bayesian Estimation)

The state-of-the-art solution for high-noise environments. It uses a continuous predict-and-update loop to separate the "true" hidden brain state from massive observational noise, vastly outperforming linear models in recognizing true state transitions.

3. Project Retrospective & Future Roadmap

Challenges Faced: Handling truncated multi-gigabyte OpenNeuro downloads and missing 3D spatial electrode coordinates required the engineering of robust fallback and synthetic injection pipelines during prototyping.

Roadmap & Risks: Future expansions could integrate real-time EEG streaming and Deep Learning/Transformer models. The primary risk remains the high false-positive rate in highly artifacted unseen clinical environments and patient data privacy concerns.
